Within Reach — match explorer

Pick a buyer on the left — they stand in for the logged-in user, the person doing the searching. Sellers they can reach turn red on the right — seller within buyer's reach AND buyer within seller's reach. Search to list those sellers' products.

This runs the reach layer's one matching rule live over a seeded catalogue — 10 buyers, 20 sellers, 600 listings. Pick a buyer on the left and every seller on the right is scored against them: the sellers whose reach overlaps the buyer's turn red and rise to the top, the rest dim out. Search in the middle and you see only the products belonging to those reachable sellers.

It's laid out this way to make one point unmistakable: visibility is mutual, not a distance filter. A seller is reachable only when seller within buyer's reach AND buyer within seller's reach — so "reachable" is a property of the pair, and the red set changes completely when you switch buyer. A worldwide buyer lights up far-off sellers who chose to sell worldwide; a local buyer lights up the few sellers next door. The search term never widens that set — it only picks products from within it.
